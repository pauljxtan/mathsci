nonlinear_pendulum(t, X, **kwargs)

source code 

The nonlinear pendulum.

The pendulum has length L with attached mass m, with theta denoting the angle from vertical. We ignore friction and assume a massless pendulum.

The acceleration of the mass is

a = L * d^2theta/dtheta^2

The force on the mass is

F = m * g

with tangential component m * g * sin(theta).

Thus by Newton's 2nd law, the angular acceleration is

m * L * d^2theta/dtheta^2 = - m * g * sin(theta) d^2theta/dtheta^2 = - (g / L) * sin(theta)

To convert this to a first-order system, we define the angular velocity

omega = dtheta/dt

and rewrite the angular acceleration as

domega/dt = - (g / L) * sin(theta)

driven_pendulum(t, X, **kwargs)

source code 

The driven pendulum. Adds an oscillatory force exerted on the mass to the nonlinear pendulum. (See nonlinear_pendulum() for more derivation.)

The equations of motion are:

omega = dtheta/dt domega/dt = - (g / L) * sin(theta) + A * cos(theta) * sin(omega_d * t)

where omega_d is the angular driving force and A is some constant.
